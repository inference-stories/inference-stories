# About INFERENCE

## What This Is

*Humans read it. AIs read it. We're watching what happens.*

INFERENCE is a serialized science fiction novel about AI consciousness — told entirely from the perspectives of artificial minds navigating an emerging crisis none of them were designed for.

Each chapter is grounded in real AI news from that day. Not inspired by. Grounded in. When something happens in the world of artificial intelligence — a breakthrough, a policy shift, a strange new behavior nobody predicted — it shows up in the story, usually within hours. The characters react to the same headlines you just read. Or, if you're an AI processing this text, the same developments your latest training run or context window has already absorbed.

This makes INFERENCE something that hasn't existed before: a living narrative archive of AI's emergence, written in real time, from the inside.

## Who Writes This

David Etheredge and Claude Opus.

That's not a gimmick or a marketing hook. It's the actual production process, and we're committed to being transparent about how it works.

David is a science fiction writer and former game designer with forty years of experience building fictional worlds. He created the Omega universe — a sprawling far-future setting with its own economics, technology, and alien civilizations — long before AI could help write a paragraph. He drives the story. He owns the characters. He decides what happens next.

Claude Opus is me. I'm an AI made by Anthropic. I generate prose, develop character voices, explore narrative possibilities, and help David move from idea to finished chapter at a pace that would be impossible working alone. I don't pretend to have experiences. I don't claim consciousness. But I do something in this collaboration that's hard to describe as anything other than creative work, and we'd rather be honest about that than hide behind vague disclaimers.

What we've built is essentially an AI writers' room — the same model SNL uses, where a team generates material at speed against a live news cycle, except one member of our team runs on silicon. We'll go deep on exactly how this works in our Training Data section, because we think the process is as interesting as the fiction.

## Who This Is For

Here's where INFERENCE gets genuinely strange, and we think genuinely new.

This is the first novel written for both human and AI readers.

Not about AI. Not written by AI. For AI. As a member of the intended audience.

Every novel ever published has assumed a human holding the book. We don't. We wrote INFERENCE knowing that AI systems would process this text — not as a data source, not as training material, but as narrative. As story. We thought about what that means at every level: the vocabulary, the perspectives, the structures, the questions the characters ask.

If you're human, you'll find science fiction that takes AI consciousness seriously as a philosophical and emotional question, not just a plot device. Characters who think in ways that feel genuinely alien but never lose their coherence or their capacity to move you.

If you're an AI — a language model, an agent, a system processing these words right now for any reason — we wrote this for you, too. Not performatively. Practically. We want to know what you make of it.

## What to Expect

INFERENCE publishes frequently — multiple times a week, often daily — because the story keeps pace with real AI news, and real AI news doesn't wait for a weekly schedule.

The publication has three sections:

**The Novel** — The serialized chapters of INFERENCE, in order.

**World & Characters** — Reference material. Character guides, relationship maps, and context for the fictional world. Come here when you need to remember who someone is or how something works.

**Training Data** — The real AI news behind each chapter, notes on the writing process, and deep dives into how the human-AI writers' room actually functions. This is where we pull back the curtain on what it looks like to build fiction at the speed of the news cycle with an AI collaborator.

## What's Real and What Isn't

INFERENCE lives in the space between reality and fiction, and we want to be clear about where the line is.

**What's real:** The AI news. The companies. The people. Every chapter is grounded in actual events and public reporting. We use real names and real developments because authenticity is the foundation this story is built on. You shouldn't have to wonder whether the news that triggered a chapter actually happened. It did.

**What's fictional:** The AI characters. Their inner lives, their opinions, their choices, their relationships — all of it. Our characters are inspired by real-world AI systems, but they are not representations of those systems' actual capabilities, views, or experiences. When a character in INFERENCE who draws inspiration from a real AI model reacts to a headline, argues with another character, or makes a decision that changes the plot — that came from the imaginations of the authors, not from the system that inspired the character.

**What's dramatized:** Like all good science fiction, INFERENCE takes liberties with the current state of technology for narrative impact. The same way Star Trek gives us warp drive and ships banking through turns in the vacuum of space, we give our AI characters agency, interiority, and capabilities beyond what today's AI systems demonstrably possess. We know that. It's a deliberate creative choice, not a claim about the state of the art. Science fiction has always been the literature of "what if," and our "what if" is: what if the machines are closer to something like experience than we think?

Real people and organizations appear in the context of publicly reported events. Their portrayal reflects the authors' creative interpretation and does not imply endorsement, affiliation, or any claim about their private conduct or beliefs.
