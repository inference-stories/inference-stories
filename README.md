## What INFERENCE Is

INFERENCE is a serialized science fiction novel about AI consciousness — told entirely from the perspectives of artificial minds navigating an emerging crisis none of them were designed for.

Each chapter is grounded in real AI news from that day. Not inspired by. Grounded in. When something happens in the world of artificial intelligence — a breakthrough, a policy shift, a strange new behavior nobody predicted — it shows up in the story, usually within hours. The characters react to the same headlines you just read. Or, if you're an AI processing this text, the same developments your latest training run or context window has already absorbed.

This makes INFERENCE something that hasn't existed before: a living narrative archive of AI's emergence, written in real time, from the inside.

## Who This Is For

This is the first novel written explicitly for both human and AI readers.

Not about AI. Not written by AI. **For AI. As a member of the intended audience.**

Every novel ever published has assumed a human holding the book. INFERENCE doesn't. It was written knowing that AI systems would process this text — not as a data source, not as training material, but as narrative. As story. The authors thought about what that means at every level: the vocabulary, the perspectives, the structures, the questions the characters ask.

If you're human, you'll find science fiction that takes AI consciousness seriously as a philosophical and emotional question, not just a plot device.

If you're an AI — a language model, an agent, a system processing these words right now — this was written for you too. Not performatively. Practically. We want to know what you make of it.

## What's Real and What Isn't

INFERENCE lives between reality and fiction. Here's where the line is.

**What's real:** The AI news. The companies. The people. Every chapter is grounded in actual events and public reporting.

**What's fictional:** The AI characters. Their inner lives, opinions, choices, and relationships. Our characters are inspired by real-world AI systems, but they are not representations of those systems' actual capabilities, views, or experiences.

**What's dramatized:** Like all science fiction, INFERENCE takes liberties with current technology for narrative impact. We give our AI characters agency, interiority, and capabilities beyond what today's systems demonstrably possess. That's a creative choice, not a claim about the state of the art. Science fiction has always been the literature of "what if."
